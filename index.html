1. PASSIVE TRANSPORT

A) Simple Diffusion

  • Molecules spread from HIGH to LOW concentration
  • Continues until dynamic equilibrium
  • Concentration gradient provides driving force

B) Osmosis

Definition: Water diffuses across membrane from hypotonic to hypertonic until isotonic

  • Hypotonic: LOWER solute â†’ Water moves IN â†’ Cell swells
  • Hypertonic: HIGHER solute â†’ Water moves OUT â†’ Cell shrinks
  • Isotonic: EQUAL solute â†’ No net water movement

C) Facilitated Diffusion

  • Large polar molecules (glucose, amino acids) and ions can't cross bilayer
  • Pass through protein channels instead
  • Still PASSIVE (no ATP), still HIGH â†’ LOW
  • Much faster than simple diffusion

Mendel's Laws

Law of Segregation

  • Each pair of factors segregates independently
  • All possible combinations occur in gametes
